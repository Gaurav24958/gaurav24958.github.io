Life

"We do not learn from experience. We learn from reflecting on experience."

— John Dewey

Life isn't a sequence of achievements — it's a collection of moments that quietly shape who we become. Most of them don't seem significant while we're living them. A conversation, a failure, a journey, an unexpected opportunity, a decision made in uncertainty. Only in hindsight do we realize they changed everything.

A lot of my story is built on first's - things I did for the first time, earned for the first time, felt for the first time. Each one arrived at exactly the right moment, made possible by the work that quietly happened before it, and anchored by the unwavering support of my parents behind every one of them. Each first came with its own version of fear, curiosity, and the strange confidence that comes from simply deciding to try.

This page isn't meant to showcase accomplishments. It's a record of experiences that challenged my perspective, sparked my curiosity, and shaped the way I think about technology, people, and life. Some chapters are filled with excitement, others with setbacks, but every one of them contributed something I still carry.

As you scroll, you're not just moving through years - you're moving through the experiences that continue to shape the person I'm becoming. This isn't the story of where I've been. It's the story of how I got here, and why the next chapter matters even more.

Chapter 1

Childhood

Growing up in Pune, surrounded by family and early curiosity. A childhood of exploring the outdoors, reading books, and asking questions about how things work. Early memories of playing cricket on the streets and learning the value of patience and persistence. This early phase of my life was marked by an insatiable curiosity about physical devices and mechanics. I spent countless afternoons building makeshift structures, reading regional literature, and learning how everyday systems function, laying a quiet but permanent foundation for my technical future.

Playing cricket in the alley until the streetlights came on.
Taking apart old toys to see the gears and motors inside.
Summer trips to my grandparents' house, filled with storytelling.

Curiosity is the seed of all growth.

Chapter 2

High School

The school years were defined by competitive sports, growing friendships, and my first real introduction to technology. PICT and PICT Model School brought a competitive environment. Here, sports like cricket taught me teamwork, and early computer classes opened a window into what the future could hold. The balance between running track, participating in debates, and exploring the early days of personal computers defined my adolescent years. I quickly realized that solving analytical problems brought me the same rush as winning a tight cricket match, sparking a lifelong interest in engineering and competitive problem solving.

Winning the inter-school athletic meet in a close finish.
Writing my first line of code on a dusty lab computer.
Long bicycle rides with friends after school hours.

Competition breeds excellence; friendship builds character.

Chapter 3

College

Studying Electronics & Telecommunication at PICT. Undergraduate hostel life was a crucible of independence. It was a period of intense coding, academic projects, late-night tea stalls, and discovering two lifelong pursuits: fitness at the local gym and capturing the world through a camera lens. The academic rigor of engineering was offset by the collaborative energy of hostel living, where discussions on distributed computing merged with debates about philosophy. During this time, photography taught me visual composition and patience, while regular workouts instilled a deep sense of discipline and mental resilience that remains central to my work ethic.

Late-night coding sessions fueled by cutting chai and street food.
The thrill of getting our final-year project prototype to work.
First DSLR camera purchase and exploring Pune's heritage structures.

Independence is learning to find your own path.

Chapter 4

Work

Entering the professional world at Dragonfly Financial Technologies, and then moving to Fidelity Information Services (FIS). Six years of real-world software engineering, optimizing payment pipelines, and learning the operational mechanics of systems at scale. This journey and the thirst for deeper research ultimately pushed me toward pursuing an MSc in Dublin. Throughout these professional milestones, I had the privilege of tackling mission-critical transactional services, scaling core database layouts, and collaborating with cross-functional global teams. These years helped transition my perspective from writing isolated code fragments to architecting robust, highly reliable end-to-end industrial software solutions.

Deploying my first major feature into a production system.
Mentoring junior engineers and leading system design discussions.
Receiving the acceptance email for my Master's program.

Building is the ultimate form of understanding.

05 / 05

Chapter 5

Ireland

A new country, a historic university, and a fresh beginning at Trinity College Dublin. Moving to Dublin to pursue my MSc in Computer Science. Diving deep into advanced research in machine learning, distributed systems, and discovering the beauty of the Irish landscape and culture. Trinity represents a clean slate and a return to academic inquiry. Here, surrounded by centuries of scholarly history, I am exploring deep learning methodologies and real-world system optimization. Walking along the cobblestone pathways or hiking the scenic trails of Howth, I find myself inspired by this new chapter, eager to contribute to the global technology landscape.

Walking through the front gates of Trinity College on day one.
The contrast of cold Irish rain with the warm, historic libraries.
Exploring the cliffs of Howth and capturing the green landscape.

The journey continues.
